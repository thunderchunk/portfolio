Bruiser Art Website

bruiserart.com

I made this site using Shopify the year before I decided to learn to become a web developer. Using their template, my partner and I set up our own stock art store. Although the site's skeleton is based on a Shopify template, I made extensive modifications.

The site is predominantly white and as minimal as possible, in order to let the artwork be the star. I stripped off everything flashy, animated, or distracting in deference to an austere look.

I tweaked the slideshow using some Jquery. We changed it from an obnoxious wipe to a simple fade.

I was chiefly responsible for content. We've got over five thousand pieces of artwork, each with 6 images, downloadable files, supplementary materials, copywriting, and links to up-sells and instructional blogs.

The blogs range from vintage artwork inspiration to detailed instructions on screenprint separation done the Aididas way. Lots of mom and pop shops that are our customers didn't have a single place to gather information on the best printing methods. Even the Specialty Graphic Imaging Association lacks a single repository of knowledge. Most places either grow content with their own systems, guard their methods as industrial secrets, or have a few skilled employees that don't share the information. I provided high quality vector artwork, and the instructions on how to print them.
